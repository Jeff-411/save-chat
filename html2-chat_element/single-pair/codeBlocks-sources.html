Answer
The second selector is better: 
text
.pb-[144px] .pb-lg.border-borderMain/50 {
                          display: none;
                        }
This selector is preferable for several reasons:
  1. It's more concise and easier to read, making the code more maintainable
    3
    .
  2. It avoids excessive nesting, which can lead to overly specific selectors and potential specificity issues
    6
    .
  3. It's more flexible, as it doesn't rely on the exact structure of child elements, allowing for potential changes in the HTML without breaking the styles
    1
    .
  4. It follows the principle of avoiding overly specific selectors, which is a best practice in SCSS and CSS
    3
    6
    .
  5. It's likely to have better performance, as browsers read selectors from right to left, and simpler selectors are generally faster to process
    6
    .
Remember, in SCSS, it's best to keep selectors as short and simple as possible while still achieving the desired specificity
